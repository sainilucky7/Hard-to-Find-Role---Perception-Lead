# Hard-to-Find-Role---Perception-Lead:
One of the toughest searches I’ve filled was a Perception Lead Engineer role for an autonomous driving team. The challenge was the combination of deep computer vision expertise, leadership experience, and hands‑on research background. The talent pool for this type of role is extremely limited, and most qualified candidates are passive, well‑compensated, and not actively looking.

To tackle this, I built a very targeted sourcing strategy. I started with competitor mapping across companies like Aurora, Cruise, Motional, Tesla, Torc, and other AV and robotics organizations. I also sourced heavily through GitHub, Google Scholar (filtering by citations and recent CV/AI papers), and accepted computer vision conference papers from CVPR, ICCV, ECCV, NeurIPS, and ICRA. I expanded the search into global talent pools, especially PhD candidates and researchers, to keep options open for O‑1 or L‑1 visas after one year if needed.

To reach passive candidates, I built automated follow‑up outreach sequences, which helped increase response rates over time. After a couple of follow‑ups, I asked engineering leaders to send personal outreach messages to high‑value candidates, which added credibility and significantly improved engagement.

I also tapped into technical communities on Discord, Slack, and LinkedIn groups to surface hidden talent. With all these combined efforts, I built a pipeline of 5–6 strong candidates, all meeting the technical and leadership bar.

In the end, we hired one exceptional candidate from the passive pipeline, and I secured a strong backup candidate from Europe as an O‑1 visa option. This search required creativity, persistence, and a multi‑channel sourcing strategy, but it resulted in a successful hire for a very hard‑to‑fill role
